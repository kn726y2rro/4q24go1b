How To Choose The Best Brushless Motor


Brushless motors have become increasingly popular in the hobbyist market due to their high efficiency and power output. However, with so many different types of brushless motors available on the market, it can be difficult to know which one is right for your application. In this guide, we'll go over some of the key factors to consider when choosing a brushless motor, as well as provide some recommendations based on your specific needs.


Factors To Consider When Choosing A Brushless Motor



  1. Kv Rating: The Kv rating (also known as the kv value) is a measure of the motor's speed and torque. It tells you how quickly the motor can accelerate and how much force it can produce. Higher Kv ratings generally result in faster acceleration and higher torque, while lower Kv ratings result in slower acceleration and less torque.


  2. Amperage: Amperage refers to the amount of current that the motor can handle. If you plan on using the motor with a large battery pack, you'll want to choose a motor with a high amperage rating. On the other hand, if you're using the motor with a smaller battery pack, you may not need such a high amperage rating.


  3. Cogging: Cogging occurs when the magnetic fields of the motor's rotor and stator interlock, causing the motor to slow down or stall. This can be particularly problematic in larger motors where the cogging effect is more pronounced. Motors with lower cogging effects tend to be smoother and quieter.


  4. Bearings: Bearings are important because they determine how smoothly the motor operates. High-quality bearings allow for smoother operation and longer lifespan.


  5. Motor Type: There are two main types of brushless motors - sensorless and sensor-based. Sensorless motors rely solely on the feedback from the ESC (Electronic Speed Control), whereas sensor-based motors use sensors to monitor the motor's position. Sensor-based motors tend to be more accurate and reliable, although they are typically more expensive.



Recommendations Based On Your Specific Needs


If you're looking for a general-purpose brushless motor, the Hobbywing Skywalker 60A 4300KV Brushless Motor might be a good choice. This motor has a moderate Kv rating, making it suitable for both acceleration and torque applications. Additionally, its low cogging effect makes it ideal for indoor flying.


For those looking for a motor with high performance and reliability, the Flytrex Titanium Pro 5200KV Brushless Motor might be worth considering. With a high Kv rating and low cogging effect, this motor provides excellent acceleration and torque. Its high-quality bearings ensure smooth operation and long lifespan.


In conclusion, selecting the best brushless motor depends on your specific needs and preferences. By taking into account the Kv rating, amperage, cogging, bearing quality, and type of motor, you should be able to find a motor that meets your requirements. Remember to consult with experienced flyers before making your final decision. Good luck!
