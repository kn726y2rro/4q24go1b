How to Choose the Best Impact Sockets


Impact sockets are essential tools for anyone who needs to work with power tools, such as drills and impact drivers. They are used to connect and disconnect sockets from the drive end of the tool. Impact sockets come in different sizes and types, and choosing the right one can make a big difference in the efficiency and safety of your work. In this article, we'll discuss how to choose the best impact sockets for your needs.


Types of Impact Sockets


There are two main types of impact sockets: standard and deep well. Standard impact sockets are designed to fit standard drill and impact driver chucks. They have a shallow well that allows for easy bit changes, but they may not provide enough grip on deep-grooved fasteners. Deep well impact sockets have a deeper well that provides better grip on fasteners with deep grooves. They are generally used with impact drivers, but they can also be used with drills in certain situations.


Sizes of Impact Sockets


Impact sockets come in different sizes, depending on the size of the fastener you're working with. You'll want to choose a socket that is slightly larger than the fastener head, but not so large that it's difficult to tighten. For example, if you're working with a #10 fastener, you'll want to choose an impact socket that's about 1/4" larger than the head of the fastener.


Drive Type


Impact sockets come in square drive and hex drive types. Square drive impact sockets are commonly used with drills, while hex drive impact sockets are commonly used with impact drivers. If you plan on using your impact socket with both drills and impact drivers, it's best to choose a socket that can accept both drive types.


Material


Impact sockets are made from different materials, including steel and alloy steel. Steel impact sockets are generally the most durable, but they can be more expensive than alloy steel sockets. Alloy steel impact sockets are less expensive and more durable than steel sockets, but they may not last as long as steel sockets.


Conclusion


Choosing the right impact socket for your needs can make a big difference in the efficiency and safety of your work. When choosing an impact socket, it's important to consider the size, type, drive type, and material. By following these guidelines, you can choose the best impact socket for your needs and get the job done right.
