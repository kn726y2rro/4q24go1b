How To Choose The Best Gel Pens


Gel pens have become increasingly popular among writers, artists, and students due to their smooth writing capabilities and vibrant colors. However, with so many different types of gel pens available in the market, it can be challenging to find the right one for your needs. In this guide, we'll go over some tips on how to choose the best gel pens based on your preferences and requirements.


Types Of Gel Pens


There are two main types of gel pens - water-based and alcohol-based. Water-based gel pens contain water as their primary ingredient while alcohol-based gel pens use isopropyl alcohol instead. Both types of gel pens offer unique benefits and are suitable for different purposes. Here's what you need to know about each type:


Water-Based Gel Pens


Water-based gel pens are ideal for those who want a smooth writing experience without any smudging or bleeding. They are also perfect for coloring books, planners, and journals since they don't bleed through the pages. Additionally, water-based gel pens are eco-friendly since they're refillable and recyclable. Some popular water-based gel pens include Sakura Gelly Roll, Uni Pin Fine Rollerball Pen, and Pilot Juice Gel Pen.


Alcohol-Based Gel Pens


Alcohol-based gel pens are known for their quick-drying properties, making them perfect for left-handed writers and professionals who need to quickly sign documents. They also come in a variety of colors, making them great for art projects and crafts. However, alcohol-based gel pens may cause some staining if used on certain surfaces such as fabric or leather. Popular alcohol-based gel pens include Sharpie Accent Gel Pen, Paper Mate Flair Fountain Pen, and Bic Flex Gel Pen.


Features To Look For


When choosing a gel pen, there are several features to consider. These include:


1. Ink Type: As discussed earlier, water-based vs. alcohol-based gel pens.


2. Color Selection: Consider whether you prefer a wide range of colors or a limited selection.


3. Bonding Capacity: If you plan to write on dark-colored paper or fabrics, look for a gel pen with good bonding capacity.


4. Smoothness: Check if the gel pen glides smoothly across the page.


5. Dry Time: Look for a gel pen that dries quickly to avoid smearing.


6. Durability: Opt for a high-quality gel pen that lasts longer and doesn't dry out easily.


By considering these factors, you can narrow down your options and find the best gel pen for your needs.


Conclusion


Choosing the best gel pen depends on your personal preferences and requirements. Whether you're looking for a smooth writing experience, vibrant colors, or quick drying time, there's a gel pen out there for everyone. By following our tips on how to choose the best gel pens, you can find the perfect pen for all your writing and drawing needs. Happy shopping!
