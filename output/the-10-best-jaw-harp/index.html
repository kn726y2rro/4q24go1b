How To Choose The Best Jaw Harp


Jaw harps are musical instruments that are played by placing them in your mouth and plucking the strings with your teeth. They come in various shapes and sizes, so choosing the right one can be a daunting task for some people. In this article, we will discuss some factors to consider when selecting a jaw harp.


Types Of Jaw Harps


There are two main types of jaw harps - stringed and flaged. Stringed jaw harps have strings that run through the instrument, while flage jaw harps have no strings at all. Flage jaw harps are easier to play because there is no need to tune the strings. However, they may not produce as rich of a sound as their stringed counterparts.


Size And Comfort


The size of the jaw harp you select should depend on your personal preferences. If you plan on playing the jaw harp for extended periods of time, you want something that fits comfortably in your mouth without causing pain or discomfort. A good rule of thumb is to choose a jaw harp that fits snugly against your lips without leaving too much space inside your mouth.


Sound Quality


Sound quality is another important factor to consider when choosing a jaw harp. Some jaw harps produce better sounds than others depending on the materials used in their construction. For example, wooden jaw harps tend to produce richer tones compared to plastic ones. Additionally, the number of strings on the jaw harp can affect its overall sound quality. Generally speaking, the more strings a jaw harp has, the higher the pitch range it can produce.


Price


Finally, price is always a consideration when purchasing any type of musical instrument. Jaw harps vary widely in price based on their features and quality. It's important to set a budget before shopping for a jaw harp to ensure you don't spend more than you can afford.


In conclusion, choosing the best jaw harp depends on several factors such as the type, size, comfort, sound quality, and price. By considering these factors, you can find a jaw harp that suits your needs and playing style perfectly. Happy jaw harping!
