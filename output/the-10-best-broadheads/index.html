How To Choose The Best Broadheads


Broadheads are one of the most important components of a bow hunting setup. They are the point of contact between your arrow and your target, so it's crucial to choose the right ones for your needs. In this guide, we'll go over some of the key factors to consider when choosing broadheads and provide tips on how to find the best ones for your situation.


Types Of Broadheads


There are two main types of broadheads: mechanical and fixed blade. Mechanical broadheads have blades that can be retracted and expanded using a lever or trigger mechanism. Fixed blade broadheads have blades that remain fixed in place once they're installed. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages, so let's take a closer look at each.


Mechanical Broadheads


Mechanical broadheads offer several benefits compared to fixed blade broadheads. For starters, they can expand the size of their blades after impact, which helps to increase penetration and reduce the risk of spine injury. Additionally, many models feature replaceable blades, which means you don't have to buy a whole new broadhead if one blade gets damaged during a hunt. However, there is a learning curve involved with using mechanical broadheads. It takes time to master the mechanics of deploying and retracting the blades, especially in the heat of the moment during a hunt. Also, because the blades are exposed while retracted, mechanical broadheads may not be legal in all states.


Fixed Blade Broadheads


Fixed blade broadheads are generally less expensive than mechanical broadheads, making them a cost-effective option for many hunters. They also require less maintenance since there are no moving parts to worry about. However, they tend to have smaller cutting diameters than mechanical broadheads, which means they may not penetrate as deeply into thick hide. Finally, due to the exposed blades, fixed blade broadheads may not be legal in certain states.


Factors To Consider When Choosing Broadheads


Now that we've covered the different types of broadheads, let's talk about some of the key factors to consider when choosing broadheads. These include:


1. Blade Length: The length of the blades on your broadheads should match the size of the animal you plan to hunt. Generally speaking, longer blades provide better penetration and reduce the chance of the broadhead passing through the animal. However, shorter blades are easier to see and may be preferred for close-range shots.


2. Cutting Diameter: Cutting diameter refers to the width of the hole made by the blades upon impact. A larger cutting diameter provides greater blood loss and increased chances of a clean kill. However, a too large of a cutting diameter may cause damage to the animal's internal organs.


3. Weight: Weight plays a role in accuracy and penetration. Heavier broadheads generally fly faster and provide deeper penetration. However, heavier broadheads also result in less kinetic energy transferred to the target. Lightweight broadheads are ideal for shooters with lower draw weights.


4. Blade Material: There are three common materials used to make broadhead blades - stainless steel, carbon fiber, and titanium. Stainless steel blades are durable and corrosion resistant, but they are relatively heavy. Carbon fiber blades are lightweight and durable, but they are prone to chipping. Titanium blades combine the strength of stainless steel with the light weight of carbon fiber.


5. Grain Weights: Grain weight refers to the total weight of the broadhead. This number affects both accuracy and penetration. Generally, heavier broadheads provide better penetration, but lighter heads are easier to shoot accurately.


By considering these factors and doing some research into different broadhead options, you should be able to find the best broadheads for your needs. Remember to always read reviews and test out different broadheads before committing to a purchase. Good luck!
